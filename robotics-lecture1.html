Arduino Uno

The Arduino Uno is in simple terms the brain of our car. It takes commands from us and stores it in it's memory so that it can then continously continuously do these commands.

The commands you will give the Arduino will allow the Arduino to vary the speed at which it will be moving and to change the direction it is moving in.

Before we can do that... You should first know some important parts of the Arduino.

Pins

If you look at the long sides near the edge of the Arduino, you will see 4 black bars. If you look at these bars from the top you will see small sqaure holes. These sqaure holes are called Pins. Into these pins you can insert wires that you will use to power the motor of the car. These Pins act as a positive side of a battery that will be used along with the Gnd pins, the negative side of the battery. You can control wheter or not the "battery" is on, off or partialy on. More on this later...

Not all these pins are the same. If you look next to the pins on the Arduino uno's "floor" you will see that there are letter and numbers standing there. Every pin has it's own name. At the top pins you will see that they are labeled, in order, from 0 to 13. There is also a Gnd (which you know) and a AREF which you will not care about. These pins (0-13) is refered to as the I/O pins (input/output).

At the bottom pins on the right you will see pins named from A0 to A5. These are called the Analog pins. I will explain to you what they are use for later on.

The last ones are the power pins. You will see that there is two additional Gnd pins that we can use. The pin Labeled Vin is the pin where the positive side of our battery will be connected to to power the board without it being connected to the computer (Gnd is used for the negative side of battery). There is also a 5V and 3V pin. These can get used to power other components.
