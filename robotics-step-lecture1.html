Lecture 1

Introduction

Greetings everyone, thank you all for being here today. I am Mr. Thinus and I am thrilled to see and meet you all. Today we start with your journey of becoming a critical thinker and an entrepreneur.

1. What will we be doing today

  1. Discuss what your project is.
  2. Discuss what you will learn in the coming weeks.
  3. You will see what your product of your project will look like.
  4. Discuss what will you need to do.
  5. Where can you find information?

1.1 What is your project?

You will need to start your own business. The ultimate goal of this project is to sell the product that you will create. You will need to create a business plan which you would then need to present/pitch to an outside unbiased investor. The investor might ask you a few questions which you will need to answer. After the investor have examined everyone's business proposals, he will approve at most 1/4 of them (the best ones). You cannot afford to be denied. (If asked about what happens to the other 3/4, "There is nothing I can do for you." alternate "You cannot afford to be denied.")

1.2 What will you learn in the coming weeks?

You will learn important concepts of electric circuits. We will show you how to do circuit analysis, what different components are and what they do, and the fundamental electrical quantities. You will also learn how the Arduino microcontroller works and how to program it.

1.3 Demonstration of Project

This object in my hand is a fundamental part of your project/business. You will be designing, coding, assembling and ultimately use in your business. It can move forward, reverse and change direction.

1.4 What you need to do

The first few weeks are vital if you want to complete this project. You must ensure that you understand exactly what is going on. If you are lost, ask immediately.

1.5 Information

If you need additional notes or cannot remember work that was covered in class, you should have noted it down. You can find more information on the website.
